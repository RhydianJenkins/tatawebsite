WEST GLAMORGAN CROSS COUNTRY LEAGUE - THIRD FIXTURE 2015/2016 SEASON

Sunday January 10th 2016 - 11am

Course Details - This is a Road race of approximately 6 miles. As last year, the course will be two laps of the reservoir. The Intention is to split the race again if the field exceeds 250 athletes - ladies first at 11.00am, men a few minutes later. The current plan is to run the same way round as last year – a clockwise loop of the reservoir – but it will be windy so come prepared. Please be aware of strong gusts and run well over to the left away from the edge of the reservoir.

Entry Fee - £2 for athletes registered with Welsh Athletics (or relevant athletics body) - £4 for unregistered athletes. You will be asked to show your Welsh Athletics affiliation card or £4 will be charged.

Website for more details http://westglamleague.co.uk/4.html

SPECIAL GENERAL MEETING

Date Sunday 22 November @ 10:00

Meeting Agenda To appoint Flag officers - Commodore, Vice Commodore and Treasurer.

The AGM was held on The 8th of November, there was a poor turnout of members. The meeting failed to elect members to three posts within the sailing club, those of Commodore, Vice Commodore and Treasurer. Our constitution states that without these posts filled, we are unable to function as a club and therefore we would have to dissolve and close the club. The committee of ten members is in place and has a wealth of experience, but we need three volunteers to fill the above posts.

What is their role?

The Commodore The Commodore is the figurehead of the sailing club, he or she can be have a hands on or delegatory approach. This is entirely up to the individual post holder.

The Vice Commodore The Vice Commodore is a deputy to the Commodore, normally focuses on sailing responsibilities and will usually take over as Commodore when he or she stands down.

The Treasurer The treasurer keeps the clubs accounts, paying the relevant bills and banking the subscriptions as required.

No experience of these roles is necessary as the committee has the required knowledge to guide you.

If you feel you could fulfil one of these roles, please email Eddie Morgan our outgoing Commodore, or discuss it with a committee member.

It is important that as many members as possible attend the meeting as this determine the future of our sailing Club.

2012 Regatta

OPEN MEETING

6th & 7th October 2012

Mike Hewlins gives the race brief to the 5 fleets

The 2012 Regatta was well attended with 70 entries, 35 of which were visitors, some coming from as far afield as Newquay, Hampshire and South Devon

The standard of sailing was of the highest calibre and all were challenged to a number of different wind conditions, from the Southerly Sea Breeze on Saturday, barely touching a force two, to the high end force three Easterly we had on Sunday

Five fleets were organised by the race coodinators, who did a sterlin job in organising all of the starts with two Windward/Leeward and three General Handicap races. It meant that there was always traffic to navigate through for everyone

The weather was that warm and bright, that lunch was taken on the lawns
