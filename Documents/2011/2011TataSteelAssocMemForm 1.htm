Club Rules

The Club Rules encompass the ”Constitution”, “Standing Orders”, “Sailing Instructions”,  “Sailing Sign on requirements”,  “Limitation of the Club’s Liability”, the “Training Manual”,  the “Health & Safety Policies and Procedures”, “Food Safety Management”, and other policies and procedures.  These can be found on below, on the club Noticeboards,  in the Club Booklet, or on the Website - Corussailing.org.uk.   You / your family must adhere to the Club Rules, including your responsibilities / liabilities when on and off the water.

It is for members to decide whether their craft is fit to sail in the prevailing conditions;  by launching, members confirm the boat(s) is fit for those conditions, and they and / or their youngsters are competent to sail / compete in them;  trainee members undertaking training will be advised and supervised in this regard by the club trainers on duty at that time.

The parents / guardians, or guardians nominated by them, are responsible for the safety, supervision, and behaviour of

their young person(s) on shore, and the actions of their young person(s) on the water. Parents/guardians are asked

to be at the club with their young persons, or to inform a club officer of an alternative adult who will .

Members,  parents, or guardians are entirely responsible for their own and / or their children’s safety, whether afloat or ashore, and nothing herein or within the club’s rules, practices and procedures reduces this responsibility.

Your signature on the reverse of this form is your agreement to comply with the all the Club Rules.

Safety in the Dinghy Park

Boats must be securely tied down. To reduce the risk of fire in dry months and keep the club tidy, it is your duty to keep the grass cut short in your compound area.

Boat Trailers

Road trailers are not allowed to be kept at the club, unless they are under your dinghy.

Those not renewing their membership.

If you do not renew your membership by the 31st March, you must pay a joining fee in addition to your membership fee, or you must remove your dinghy from the Club premises.  If you have difficulties here, please contact any committee member. The Club reserves the right to sell / scrap dinghies and / or trailers where membership has lapsed.
