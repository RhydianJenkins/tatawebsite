WELCOME TO WINDSURFERS

You are allowed to sail up to six times as a visitor, after that you must join the club;  visitors must complete a “day sailing form” and complete the “sailing register” before taking to the water.  The day sailing fee for 2016 will be £10 per visit.

Whatever your ability, the Lake is ideal for windsurfing in most wind directions, with a spacious grassy area for rigging, and an easy launching area.  You don’t have to worry about tides and you can enjoy the luxury of hot showers, hot drinks and the chance to have a chat with other members.

The lake is open for windsurfing on Sundays only from October to mid March;  in the Spring and Summer we’re also open on Saturdays and Tuesday and Wednesday evenings (see club booklet). 

Its important that you hold the combination lock closed and spin the numerals when entering or exiting the reservoir outer gate.

There are a few safety rules which MUST be observed for your own and other water users’ safety:

  • You can only sail when there is safety boat cover – at the moment this is at the times above, but if we can get enough people interested and trained to operate the safety boat then we can sail anytime as long as safety boat cover is provided
  • You must wear a buoyancy aid (a harness does not count!) – this is a requirement of the lakes’ owners.
  • You must have Third Party insurance of £3m.

Because of  a problem with an invasive species of shrimp, also found in Cardiff Bay and a few other locations, you must wash your board, sail, and wetsuit before leaving the reservoir (see HS&E page).  The shrimp are harmless to people, but they are predators of other brackish and freshwater life, so it is really important that they do not spread to other waters.

Having got the boring bits over, hopefully we can look forward to some windy weather.

Calling All Windsurfers

    Hi All …

    My association with Tata sailing club started way back in 1984 (then British Steel Sailing Club) when I joined as a young teenager sailing a mirror dinghy with my father Stan Williams.  My enthusiasm for dinghy sailing grew and I went on to win a number of club championships in various dinghy classes.  I also became an active member of the flourishing RYA Welsh youth training scheme, often attending training camps at Llangorse and Plas Menai

During the 90’s university life and my career took me away from the South Wales area but my desire for sailing continued in both dinghies and yachts.  During the early 00’s the pull to return home was too great to ignore and I re-established my association with Tata sailing club (then Corus Sailing Club).

When Tata relaxed its rules around windsurfing at the reservoir in 2009 I decided I needed a new challenge after many years of dinghy racing so took the plunge.  Although new to the sport of windsurfing I have a great association (and many good memories) with the sailing club which has spanned nearly 3 decades. 

I look forward to the challenge of windsurfing rep and hope my lengthy knowledge of the club can help drive windsurfing forward at Tata …

Look forward to meeting you all … Mike Williams.
