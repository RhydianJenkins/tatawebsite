    Solo Welsh Championship
    5/6 May 2012

    Tata Sailing Club was the venue of the 19th year running of the Solo Welsh Championship. 34 Soloists came to the line this consisted of 13 home boats and 21 visitors. Thank you to all who attended.

    On Saturday the wind was not very kind with light and fluky winds from the eas t which is over the mountains, this caused the race officer Iain Williams much consternation trying to get a true start line and beat.

    = p>

    Once the racing was underway Steve Denison appeared to get to grips better than most counting a 1, 3, 1 for the day and was the overnight leader closely followed by Peter Mitchell counting 3, 4, 2 and laying second overnight. In third place was a Chris Brown with a 2, 1, 8.

    After the usual festivity on the Saturday night in the local hostelry where much banter was taking place Sunday dawned with a much better wind direction. With the wind blowing from the South East for the fourth race there was a much longer beat and with a the first reach along the front of the clubhouse with much banter from the spectators, unfortunately for Steve he could not repli cate his Saturdays results and Peter lead from start to finish followed by local Bob Taylor and Chris Brown tying up third spot. This left the championship down to three helms on the last race.

    Under a black flag the fifth and last race got underway wind was now blowing from the South but shifting all over the place. Overnight favourite Steve Denison was on the wrong side of the start line and was disqualified this finished his championship expectations. This left it to Pete and Chris but neither was up the front of the pack on the first rounding Gareth Griffiths lead from start to finish with much place changing behind peter coming up to fifth while Chris caught up to an important fourth.

    First place was tied with Pete’s best four results counting 3,4,2,1 and Chris’s best four  2,1,3,4 as they were tied it was all on the last race which handed the title to Chris Brown from the RYA.
