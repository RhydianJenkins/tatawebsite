Club Rules: The Club rules can be found in the Rules, Policies, Procedures and Advice folder located in the office.

Extracts are also shown on the club Noticeboards, in the Club Booklet, or on the Website. You, and your family members if applicable, must adhere to these club rules, including your responsibilities / liabilities when on and off the water. Your signature on the other side of this form confirms your agreement to comply with the all the Club Rules.

It is for members to decide whether the craft they intend to sail is fit for the prevailing conditions;  by launching, members confirm the craft(s) is fit for those conditions, and they and / or their dependants are competent to sail / compete in them;  trainee members undertaking training will be advised and supervised in this regard by the club instructors on duty at that time.

Parents, including guardians, or persons nominated by them, are responsible for the onshore safety, supervision, and behaviour of  their child / young person(s) under 18, and the actions and behaviour of their dependants on the water. Parents  / Guardians are required to be at the club with their dependants, or to inform a club officer of an alternative adult who will.  Members, parents / guardians are entirely responsible for their own and / or their childs / young persons safety, whether afloat or ashore, and nothing herein or within the clubs rules, practices and procedures reduces this responsibility.

 

Safety in the Dinghy Park: Boats must be securely tied down (see specification in the club rules), and to reduce the risk of fire in dry months and keep the club tidy, it is your duty to keep the grass cut short in your compound area.

 

Boat Trailers: Road trailers are not allowed to be kept at the club, unless they are under your dinghy.

Working Parties: To maintain the club and its facilities it is expected that members will take part in working parties. Please note any personal skills here:-

 

Those not renewing their membership.

If you do not renew your membership by the 31st March, you must pay a joining fee in addition to your membership fee, or you must remove your dinghy(s) from the Club premises.  If you have difficulties here, please contact the membership Secretary on 01639 767052 membership@tatasteelsailing.org.uk  The Club reserves the right to dispose of your craft on club premises when membership lapses, and account to you for any balance due, after outstanding fees have been deducted.
