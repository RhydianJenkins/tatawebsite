A club that offers both year round high standard competitive racing alongside family friendly sail training in a nurturing environment. Tata Steel sailing club, Margam shows how it is all possible…..

 

Sailing:

 

Tata Steel sailing club is a friendly and welcoming club open to both employees and non-employees of Tata Steel Works.  Having undergone significant building improvements in recent months, the club offers superb changing and toilet facilities, a warm and comfortable clubhouse and galley, external race hut and a new boat shed for powerboats, boat repairs and sail storage.

 

Recently it has been agreed that windsurfing shall now be allowed on the reservoir as part of Tata sailing club. Anyone interested in this should see the club website for details.

 

Location:

 

Tata steel sailing club is located just off junction 38 of the M4 in South Wales

 

To gain access to the reservoir, a gate combination code is required. This is given to members when subscriptions are paid. As a visitor to the club, please ring the club house on 07951553488 and a member will join you at the gate to let you in!

History:

 

Tata sailing club has been in existence since 1953 as part of Tata Steels sports and social club section. Originally known as the British steel sailing club, the club moved to it’s current location on the shores of Egleys Nunydd reservoir in 1963. Since then the club has kept it’s members busy with a full racing and training programme.

 

Racing:

 

Thanks to the lack of tide, Racing takes place for members:

 

Throughout the winter with 2 races every Sunday

Through out the summer with 3 Races every Sunday, Wednesday evenings and Midday on Saturdays.

 

With additional regattas held on bank holidays.

Visitors are welcome to take part in our Solo open (May), the October regatta (First weekend in October) and in other advertised events.

 

 

Cruising:

 

Tuesday evenings are generally reserved for casual sailing, although during this time members are available to give direction and pointers to improve your sailing technique.

 

 

 

Social:

 

Throughout the summer Tata Steel sailing club hosts a series of summer evening barbeques as well as sailing open days enabling the whole family to be involved with the club.

 

Training:

 

Tata steel sailing club has been awarded RYA training centre status and currently teaches RYA level 1 and 2 courses for adults, Stages 1 – 4 on the youth sailing scheme and Powerboat levels 1-3 including safety boat.

The club has its own fleet of teaching and training boats allowing new members to get straight on the water.

In addition to this our long standing mirror sailors offer tuition in rigging and sailing Mirror dinghies throughout the summer on Tuesday evenings.

 

2012 sees the launch of the RYA Onboard scheme at the club, linking local primary and secondary schools with the sailing experience in the form of taster sessions through to courses of lessons following the RYA National Youth Sailing Scheme
