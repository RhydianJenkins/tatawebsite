Corus Sailing Club, Margam

 

Laser and Enterprise Open Meeting

Eglwys Nunydd Reservoir, Margam, Port Talbot

Sunday 11 June 2006

Notice of Race

 

1.      Organising Authority

1.1       The Organising Authority is Corus Sailing Club, Margam (the Sailing Section of Corus Sports and Social Club, Margam).  

 

2.      Rules

2.1                   Racing will be governed by the ‘Rules’ as defined in The Racing Rules of Sailing and the Sailing Instructions.  This Notice of Race may be amended by a Notice to Competitors from the Organising Authority.  If there is any conflict with a Sailing Instruction, the Sailing Instruction shall prevail.

 

3.      Classes and eligibility

3.1                   Racing will be in the International Laser class, and the Enterprise class.

 

4.      Entries

4.1                   Entries should be submitted on the official entry form for this event along with a cheque payable to BSSC :-

                         Single hander           £ 8                  

                         Double hander          £ 12.              

            The entry fee for competitors under the age of 18 on 11 June will be reduced by 50%.

Entry forms will be available from the Club on the day.

 

5.      Registration

Competitors will register at the Clubhouse on 11 June, from 9.30 a.m.

 

6.      Measurement

6.1                   All boats must have a valid measurement certificate, and a buoyancy endorsement if required by class rules.

 

7.      Sailing Instructions & Briefing

7.1                   Sailing Instructions will be issued at registration.

7.2                   There will be a competitors’ briefing at 10.30 on 11 June at the Race Control Hut.

7.3                   Notices to competitors will be posted at the Race Control Hut.

 

8.      Racing Programme

Three races are scheduled :
      Race 1       11.00 a.m.                 Races 2 and 3 to be announced.

 

9.        Courses and Racing Areas

9.1                   The racing areas will be announced at the briefing. Courses will be described on the courseboard at the Race Control Hut or from the Committee Boat as announced.

 

10.   Scoring

10.1                The event will be scored according to RRS Appendix A. Each boat’s series score will be the total of her race scores with her worst score excluded if 3 races have been completed.

 

11.   Coaches and Supporters

11.1                Only official patrol boats appointed by the principal race officer may:-

a.           Communicate with competitors afloat

b.           Enter the race area.

 

12.   Rights to use names and likenesses

12.1                Competitors automatically grant to the Organising Authority without payment the right in perpetuity to make, use and show any motion pictures, still pictures and live, taped or filmed television of or relating to the event.  

 

13.   Disclaimer of Liability

13.1                Competitors are entirely responsible for their own safety, whether afloat or ashore, and nothing reduces this responsibility. It is for competitors to decide whether their boat is fit to sail in the conditions in which it will find itself. By launching competitors confirm the boat is fit for those conditions and that they are competent to sail and compete in them. Nothing done by the organisers can reduce the responsibility of the owners and/or competitors, nor will it make the organisers responsible for any loss, damage, death or personal injury, however it may have occurred, as a result of the boat taking part in the racing. The organisers encompass everyone helping to run the event. The provision of patrol boats does not relieve owners and competitors of their responsibilities.

 

13.2      Each sailor under the age of 18 on 11 June will be required to submit a consent
form signed by a parent or guardian.

 

14.   Safety

14.1                All competitors must wear adequate personal buoyancy of an appropriate standard while afloat. Wet suits and dry suits do not constitute adequate personal buoyancy.

 

15.   Insurance

Boats are required to be adequately insured including insurance against third party claims of at least £2,000,000.
